into the deep (2024-2025)

robot v2 - complete redesign
december 2024 - march 2025

for this redesign of our team's itd robot, i addressed the main concerns with the original robot by directly attaching the lift to the drivetrain instead of using a separate plate and using a wheel/spool assembly instead of a linkage. i also added a lot more crossbeams for stability.

carousel wheel / spool

due to the complexity and unreliability of the drv4b, i opted to change it to a string-driven assembly, similar to that of the vertical extension but driven with servos. the spool had to be driven by servos due to the eight-motor limit imposed on ftc robots, and the weight of the slide assembly needed to be under a certain amount. the problem with the servos was that they could not output enough rpm to move the extendo at the speed required for the high cycles throughout each match, so i used a series of inverse kinematics equations in comparison to a motor of 1150 rpm to calculate the gear ratio necessary to achieve as close to the speed needed.

arm / claw assembly

along with gaining another degree of freedom for the rotation of the claw, the design of the claw was changed to one that was open-sourced by gear wizards, ftc team 16917. additionally, the arm was compacted and driven by one servo motor instead of two for less weight.

inverse kinematics string drive servo control weight optimization
robot v1 - initial design
september 2024 - december 2024

my overall design for this year's game was to have an extendo on the lift that would be able to intake from both the front and back of the robot to score the scoring elements. this is the first version of the robot i designed for the game, as my team later did a rebuild.

double reverse virtual four-bar (drv4b)

this design was inspired by ftc team 9614 hyperion during their centerstage game, and i chose this linkage for its range of motion, being able to reach the back and front of its pivot point. however, it was bulky and required too much torque. its main issue was being cantilevered / not constrained well, causing the first stage in the linkage to often not rotate the gears at the top. for the first qualifier of the year, we replaced this linkage with a two-bar for reliability.

slides assembly

stringing system

for linear vertical extension, i used a complex rigging system that only relies on one motor for each side to retract and extend. the main issues it had were with tensioning and stability due to the plate the slides were mounted on being unstable.

drv4b linkage complex rigging dual-direction intake
robot in two days
september 2024

in robot in two days, my team and i decided on having a telescoping mechanism using slides. i designed the pivot mechanism using 3d printed gears and included a loony claw on the end of our slides. instead of modeling the drivetrain, i decided to measure the distances i needed and put together the parts on the cad based on those measured distances. the main issue was that i was unfamiliar with stringing, and the stringing didn't work.

swerve drive system
june 2024 - august 2024

for a team summer project, i spent two to three weeks making 5 different cad designs until i settled on one that i felt was satisfactory and built it. the hardest challenge of designing the swerve was creating one that was compact and though i wish i could've compacted it more, i am satisfied with the look of it. i designed each pod to be modular for easy assembly and integration with the other parts of the robot and i enjoyed creating a battery holder that connects the back two swerve pods and constrains the battery via a sliding plate that is held in by two pins.

swerve drive modular design iterative development custom mounting
lightweight intake
january 2024 - june 2024

due to the weight of the original flip-down intake, i designed a new intake that was lighter due to having the ramp attached to the drivetrain rather than being a part of the intake and the plates were all made out of pla plastic. additionally, to prevent dragging, the intake would hover over the pixels. there are guides on the ramp and a cover on the top of the intake to prevent the pixels from getting stuck. its main issue was that it was hard to intake from preset stacks.

weight optimization pla construction anti-jam design
precision claw
december 2023

to solve the inaccuracy of the bucket design my team used to hold the scoring elements, i made a cad of a claw design which was one of my first few cad projects on my team. the claw arms were to be passively tied by rubber bands to the screws above it and two servos would push the claw arms down into the center of the pixel to hold it in place. i was inspired by tehnoz. though prototyped and proven to work, it required too much change on the robot then to implement.

hexapod walking robot

six-legged walking robot
personal project

complete hexapod robot designed from scratch featuring custom inverse kinematics, dynamic gait patterns, and modular leg assemblies for stable locomotion across varied terrain.

view on github

mechanical design

each leg features three degrees of freedom (coxa, femur, tibia) for complex movement patterns. body structure provides stable electronics platform while maintaining low center of gravity. custom servo mounting brackets reduce mechanical stress during operation.

kinematics & control

implemented inverse kinematics algorithms translating desired foot positions into servo angles for smooth, natural motion. supports multiple gait patterns: tripod gait for speed and wave gait for stability. real-time joint angle calculation maintains static stability with minimum three legs in ground contact.

inverse kinematics gait planning servo control 3d printing real-time control
