into the deep (2024-2025)

robot v2 - complete redesign
december 2024 - march 2025

addressed main concerns from v1 by directly attaching the lift to the drivetrain and replacing the drv4b linkage with a wheel/spool assembly. added extensive crossbeaming for stability.

carousel wheel / spool system

transitioned from the complex drv4b to a string-driven assembly operated by servos due to ftc's eight-motor limit. the challenge was that servos couldn't achieve the required rpm for high-cycle operation. solved this using inverse kinematics equations compared to a 1150 rpm motor to calculate the optimal gear ratio.

arm / claw assembly

gained an additional degree of freedom for claw rotation. adopted an open-source design from gear wizards (ftc 16917). compacted the arm and reduced from two servo motors to one for weight savings.

inverse kinematics string drive servo control weight optimization
robot v1 - initial design
september 2024 - december 2024

first iteration featuring an extendo on the lift capable of intaking from both front and back. included innovative drv4b linkage and complex stringing system.

double reverse virtual four-bar (drv4b)

inspired by ftc team 9614 hyperion, this linkage offered excellent range of motion reaching both sides of the pivot point. however, it proved bulky and torque-intensive. main issue was cantilevering causing the first stage to fail rotating the top gears. replaced with a two-bar for first qualifier reliability.

stringing system

complex rigging using only one motor per side for vertical extension. main challenges were tensioning and stability due to unstable slide mounting plate.

drv4b linkage complex rigging dual-direction intake

hexapod walking robot

six-legged walking robot
personal project

complete hexapod robot designed from scratch featuring custom inverse kinematics, dynamic gait patterns, and modular leg assemblies for stable locomotion across varied terrain.

view on github

mechanical design

each leg features three degrees of freedom (coxa, femur, tibia) for complex movement patterns. body structure provides stable electronics platform while maintaining low center of gravity. custom servo mounting brackets reduce mechanical stress during operation.

kinematics & control

implemented inverse kinematics algorithms translating desired foot positions into servo angles for smooth, natural motion. supports multiple gait patterns: tripod gait for speed and wave gait for stability. real-time joint angle calculation maintains static stability with minimum three legs in ground contact.

inverse kinematics gait planning servo control 3d printing real-time control
